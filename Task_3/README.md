# Task 3: Analytics & Commercial Application

## Overview

This task focuses on transforming analytical findings into a professional business presentation for the client. The objective is to communicate complex analytical results in a clear, concise, and actionable manner that supports strategic decision-making.

The presentation summarizes the key findings from the previous analytical tasks and provides commercial recommendations based on customer purchasing behaviour and trial store performance.

---

## Business Problem

The Category Manager requires an executive-level report that answers the following questions:

- What are the key customer purchasing trends within the Chips category?
- Which customer segments contribute the most to category sales?
- Was the in-store trial successful?
- Which findings should influence the upcoming category strategy?
- What business actions are recommended based on the analysis?

The final deliverable should communicate these insights in a format suitable for business stakeholders with minimal technical jargon.

---

## Objectives

The presentation was developed to:

- Summarize findings from Task 1 and Task 2
- Present business insights using effective data visualization
- Apply the Pyramid Principle for structured communication
- Translate analytical findings into commercial recommendations
- Support strategic decision-making through evidence-based insights

---

## Key Business Insights

The presentation highlights several important findings:

- Overall category sales remained relatively stable throughout the analysis period.
- Mainstream customers contributed the largest share of sales across several customer segments.
- Family households represented an important customer group within the Chips category.
- Store 77 demonstrated the strongest positive trial outcome compared to its matched control store.
- Stores 86 and 88 showed moderate and limited trial impacts respectively.
- Customer growth was identified as the primary driver of improved sales performance during the trial.

---

## Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations were presented:

- Prioritize marketing strategies for high-value customer segments.
- Consider expanding the successful trial strategy to stores with characteristics similar to Store 77.
- Conduct further investigation before implementing the same strategy across stores similar to Store 88.
- Continue monitoring customer purchasing behaviour to support future category planning.
